Open Ag Data Alliance (OADA) Privacy and Use Principles

OADA Reference Implementation Example

Privacy and Use Component (PUC) Description
Modular Choice and Consent The farmer has a choice to opt-out of sharing data with an entity or with third parties if that sharing is not the primary purpose of the service or product.
Third Party Audit and Certification An independent third party has certified that the internal privacy and use practices of an entity coincide with an entity’s stated policy, and has verified each assertion of true/false for these OADA principles.
Deletion of Data Upon Request A farmer’s data is deleted upon the farmer’s request, with reasonable considerations for backup and recovery services. A particular farmer’s contribution to aggregated data is impossible to delete in general once it has become non-identifiable, so this principle refers only to data which is possible to identify and delete.
Publicly accessible policies The privacy and use policies of a given entity and the assertions of true/false for each of these OADA principles are publicly available, and their locations are properly provided as described in the OADA API.
Sharing of Identifiable Data with Third Parties A farmer explicitly controls who can see his or her data, and identifiable data shared with this entity is not provided to outside parties unless requested by the farmer. This includes personally identifiable data as well as geospatially identifiable data.
Sharing of Anonymized or Aggregate Data with Third Parties Anonymous or aggregate data are not shared with third parties unless requested by the farmer.
Internal Guidelines for Data Access A company has internal policies which limit access to farmer data to only those individuals and uses necessary to properly provide a farmer with a given service.
Access and Transferability A farmer and his authorized agents are not intentionally denied access to the farmer’s data, and the farmer can transfer his or her data from a service at any time.
Open Formats All data generated for a farmer is available to that farmer in a publicly published format.
